About

Kathrin Byard is a Front End Developer from the Sacramento area. She is experienced in HTML, CSS, Git, Bootstrap, Front End JavaScript, jQuery, JavaScript Algorithms, AJAX, and Node.js.

Kathrin graduated from California State University, Sacramento with a BA in Economics, and also has four Associate Degrees. In addition to web development, she has worked in the environmental field, and has many years of experience in accounting, business administration, and human resources.

You can browse some of Kathrin’s projects below. Please feel free to contact her with any questions, or if you are interested in utilizing her skills on your own projects, using the links at the bottom of the page.

Clicky Game: Doctor Who?!

Who's your favorite Doctor? Try to keep them straight in this React-based memory game. This project breaks up the application's UI into components, manages component state, and responds to user events.

All the News That's Fit to Scrape

A scraper application which captures article information from the New York Times. Displays title, summary, and image of NYT articles. Users can search titles for keywords, save their preferred articles, and add and edit notes on articles.



L'Attaque!

General Quarters, General Quarters; All Hands Man Your Battle Stations! This collaborative project combines front-end styling with backend databases like Firebase and Heroku to create a fun game of skill and strategy.

Friend Finder

A compatibility-based friend-finding application! This full-stack site takes in results from your survey, then compare your answers with those from other users, and then display your best overall match.

Bamazon

An Amazon-like storefront app created with MySQL and Node.js. The app takes in orders from customers and depletes stock from the store's inventory.

LIRI: SIRI's Lesser-Known Cousin

LIRI is a command line node app that takes in parameters and gives back data. Similar to iPhone's SIRI, except it is a Language Interpretation and Recognition Interface instead of a Speech Interpretation and Recognition Interface.



Endcap

This collaborative project combines information from various APIs to help those looking to relocate to a specific area find the resources they need.

[Trivia] Game of Thrones

When you play the [Trivia] Game of Thrones, you win or you die! …well, ok, maybe it’s not that dramatic. This trivia game application uses JavaScript for the logic and jQuery to manipulate HTML.

Crystal Collector

A fun and interactive game for web browsers! This application dynamically updates its HTML with the jQuery library.
